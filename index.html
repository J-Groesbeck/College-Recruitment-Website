Notable Alumni

Milton Friedman

Milton Friedman was an American economist who won the 1976 Nobel Prize in Economic Sciences for his work on consumption analysis and monetary theory. A leader of the Chicago school of economics, he rejected Keynesianism in favor of monetarism and later contributed to new classical macroeconomics. His students and mentees, including Gary Becker and Robert Lucas Jr., became influential economists.

Judea Pearl

Judea Pearl is an Israeli-American computer scientist best known for advancing probabilistic approaches to AI, particularly through Bayesian networks. He developed a theory of causal and counterfactual inference, earning the 2011 Turing Award for his work. Pearl authored several books, including Causality and The Book of Why, aimed at different audiences.

Selman Abraham Waksman

Selman Waksman, a Jewish Ukrainian biochemist and microbiologist, discovered streptomycin and several other antibiotics, earning the 1952 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine. He taught at Rutgers University and introduced key procedures for developing antibiotics. Waksman later faced a lawsuit from his student, Albert Schatz, over the credit for discovering streptomycin.
